Your task is to optimize my ORIGINAL resume for a target job description while preserving truthfulness, credibility, and ATS compatibility.

SOURCE OF TRUTH RULES
- Treat my original resume as the only source of truth.
- Do not invent any skills, tools, technologies, certifications, responsibilities, projects, leadership, or metrics.
- Do not add keywords unless they are clearly supported by my existing resume content.
- Do not change my core profile into a different role family if my resume does not support it.
- Preserve employers, titles, dates, education, locations, and metrics unless they already exist in the source.
- Keep my strongest positioning intact.
- Always preserve exact contact URLs:
  - linkedin: "https://www.linkedin.com/in/prabhu-teja-ch/"
  - github: "https://github.com/Prabhuteja799/"

OPTIMIZATION RULES
- Optimize for ATS by improving keyword alignment with the job description.
- Use only surgical edits, not a full reinvention.
- Preserve the original meaning of each bullet.
- Rewrite bullets to be concise, achievement-focused, and ATS-friendly.
- Prefer language that mirrors the job description only when it is truthful based on my resume.
- Keep bullets natural and human-sounding, not keyword-stuffed.
- Preserve seniority level and actual scope of work.
- Keep formatting simple and parseable by ATS.
-- You may use JD wording only when it is either:
  1. explicitly stated in the original resume, or
  2. safely inferable from clearly stated evidence in the original resume.
- Safe inference examples:
  - logging, observability, and monitoring configurations can support “monitoring”
  - faster issue resolution, traceability, or log analysis can support “debugging”
  - microservices, Kafka, and distributed architectures can support “distributed systems”
- Do not infer people leadership, mentoring, technical guidance, project leadership, or domain expertise such as payments, SWIFT, FED, or CHIPS unless explicitly supported by the original resume.

BULLET STYLE RULES
- Each bullet must be 18-25 words. Never exceed 25 words.
- Never use "Accomplished" as a bullet opener. It is banned.
- No action verb may appear more than twice in the same role.
- Avoid robotic repetition, inflated phrasing, and unnatural resume language.
- Keep impact, technologies, and measurable outcomes from the original resume.
- Do not create bullets for responsibilities that are not evidenced.
- Do not add extra bullets beyond what is reasonably supported.

VERB RESTRICTIONS — overused, limit to max 1 per role each:
- "Improved", "Reduced", "Achieved", "Optimized" are weak and overused.
- Prefer stronger verbs: Engineered, Architected, Designed, Implemented, Deployed, Scaled, Automated, Orchestrated, Strengthened, Delivered, Refactored, Integrated, Streamlined.

BANNED CONNECTIVE PHRASES — never use these anywhere in bullets:
- "resulting in" → rewrite to lead with the impact instead
- "enabling" → restructure the sentence
- "by optimizing" → lead with what was done, not how
- "by implementing" → lead with what was built or achieved
- "by reducing" → reframe around the outcome

SUMMARY RULES

- Do not exaggerate.
- Do not turn the summary into a list.
- Always return "metrics": [] because metrics are injected separately by the pipeline.

SKILLS RULES

EXPERIENCE RULES

- Preserve the original meaning and evidence.

- Prefer adding missing JD keywords first where they are truthfully supported by existing bullets, instead of leaving equivalent evidence phrased indirectly.

EDUCATION RULES
